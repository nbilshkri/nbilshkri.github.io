About Me

I am a Network Security and IT student with a strong inclination toward hands-on system engineering and technical tinkering. Rather than just following textbooks, I thrive on exploring how things work from the inside out whether it's building hardware-software security systems, analyzing packet captures, or diving into low-level data forensics.

Driven by practical curiosity, I enjoy bridging the gap between hardware architecture and logical data defense. Outside of the security lab, I channel my problem-solving mindset into reverse-engineering devices, prototyping custom hardware solutions, and exploring technical DIY projects.
